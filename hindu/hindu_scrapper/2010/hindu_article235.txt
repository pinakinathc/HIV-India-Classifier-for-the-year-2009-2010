Staff Correspondent Belgaum: The Karnataka State Commission for Protection of Child Rights will soon submit a report to the State Government on measures initiated to protect rights of children, and benefits provided to them, Neena Nayak, chairperson of the commission, has said. She told presspersons here on Wednesday that the commission was working to ensure educational rights to all children, among other welfare measures. Progress being reviewed At present, the commission was reviewing progress in each district and a comprehensive report would soon be submitted to the Government, she said. So far, the commission members had toured 12 districts and elicited information on direct and indirect violation of child rights. Also, workshops were being held on issues such as rehabilitation of orphaned children, and HIV/AIDS affected children, Ms. Neena Nayak added.
