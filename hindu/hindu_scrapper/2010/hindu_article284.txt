Manipur Chief Minister Okram Ibobi Singh with Deputy Chairperson of the Planning Commission Montek Singh Ahluwalia in New Delhi on Wednesday. -- Â   Special Correspondent NEW DELHI: The Annual Plan outlay for Manipur for 2010-11 was on Wednesday finalised at Rs. 2,600 crore at a meeting here between Planning Commission Deputy Chairman Montek Singh Ahluwalia and Chief Minister Okram Ibobi Singh. During the discussions, Mr. Ahluwalia lauded the State Government for stepping up the pace of development and assured the Chief Minister that funds constraint would not be allowed to hamper the growth process. Asking the State to speed up power sector reforms and improve its performance in execution of the Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana, he said the Commission would ensure that there was no delay in the release of funds for such development programmes. In this regard, it was pointed out that in recognition of the need for infrastructure development the Commission has agreed to focus attention to sectors such as road, power, education and health services while funds were also being provided to improve employment opportunities. Alongside, the Commission pointed to the fall in allocation to social services and the high incidence of HIV cases in Manipur. Briefing the Commission on the State's performance, the Chief Minister said that efforts were on to increase foodgrain production, expand the crop area under horticulture and improve processing facilities, increase the State's per capita income to the all-India level and electrify all villages. Towards this end, he said, the government intended to construct buildings for all schools during the year and work towards electrification of all villages and self-sufficiency in food by 2012.
