Staff Reporter HYDERABAD: A 32-year-old man affected by HIV, who was allegedly making and selling fake educational certificates for his medical expenses, was arrested by the Cyberabad police on Friday. He told the police that doctors diagnosed him HIV positive five years ago. With no money for medical treatment, he wanted to end his life when he met Raghu of Achampet in Mahbubnagar district in 2007. Through Raghu, he learnt the techniques of making fake educational certificates. the Balanagar ACP, K. Sridhar Rao, told reporters. On a tip-off, the Special Operations Team raided a house and arrested the man and his associate, A. Mallesh, at Tilak Nagar in Nallakunta. A computer, colour printer, 114 stamps of educational institutions were seized.
