: State Bank of India presented a cheque for Rs.70,000 to the district administration to meet the needs of children afflicted by HIV disease. According to a press release, Collector C. Kamaraj said that the contribution from the public sector bank, on the Children's Day, would be utilised for the benefit of 250 children fighting the dreaded disease in the district for buying medicines and other essential commodities. Commending the SBI's gesture, he appealed to other institutions and philanthropists' to come forward in helping such community. The bank's regional manager Ramanujam, Manager Kumar, Aids control society district coordinator Thangapandian among others were present, the release added.
