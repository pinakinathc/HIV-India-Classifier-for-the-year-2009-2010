Harish Kumar, District Programme Manager, District AIDS Prevention and Control Unit, handing over the mobile blood bank van key on behalf of TANSACS to Megalai, Superintendent of the Government Vellore Medical College Hospital, recently. — Photo: Handout    The Tamil Nadu State AIDS Control Society (TANSACS) donated a mobile blood bank van worth Rs.10 lakh to the Government Vellore Medical College Hospital (GVMCH) under a scheme, which was inaugurated by the Health Minister M.R.K. Panneerselvam at the TANSACS office in Chennai recently. Harish Kumar, District Programme Manager, District AIDS Prevention and Control Unit (DAPCU), Vellore and P. Subramaniam, District Supervisor, DAPCU handed over the van on behalf of TANSACS to Megalai, Superintendent of GVMCH last week. Dr. Harish Kumar said that the van would facilitate mobilisation of blood collected from the sites of blood donation camps to the hospital. The van, which has all the necessary infrastructure for preserving blood, could be used to transport about 400 units of blood to any other hospital in case of emergencies, he said.
