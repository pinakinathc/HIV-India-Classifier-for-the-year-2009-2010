Special Correspondent VISAKHAPATNAM: Vice-Chancellor of Andhra University B. Satyanarayana inaugurated the GFATM 7 AU Centre at the Department of Psychology and Parapsychology on Wednesday. The WHO has chosen Andhra University for capacity and infrastructure building and world class training and counselling component to reach the AIDS counsellors for effective clinical functioning, according to a press release. The Global Fund to fight AIDS, Tuberculosis and Malaria (GFATM) was established and the project completed six rounds. During the seventh round the project invited higher educational institutions to participate in the counselling sub-component to take up training and public awareness programmes. Tata Institute of Social Sciences (Mumbai) if the principal recipient of the global fund for India and will coordinate the overall implementation of the project.
